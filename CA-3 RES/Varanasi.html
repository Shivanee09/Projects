About Varanasi


Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest living cities in the world, with a history that dates back over 3,000 years. Located on the banks of the River Ganges in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, Varanasi is the spiritual heart of India and a revered pilgrimage site for millions of Hindus. Every street and ghat (riverbank step) in Varanasi tells a story of devotion, tradition, and timeless culture. The city is famous for its ancient temples, especially the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. The daily Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, with chanting, fire lamps, and the ringing of bells, creates a mesmerizing spiritual experience. Varanasi is also a hub for learning, music, and art. It has produced legendary classical musicians, poets, and scholars. The city’s narrow lanes bustle with life — from flower vendors to sadhus, from silk weavers to boatmen — making it a kaleidoscope of Indian culture. A visit to Varanasi is not just a journey through a city, but a journey into the soul of India, where the sacred and the everyday coexist in harmony.
